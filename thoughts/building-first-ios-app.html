What I learned building my first iOS app from scratch

June 2025 · 5 min read · iOS SwiftUI

Six months ago I had never written a line of Swift. Today I have two apps in various states of completion and a long list of things I wish someone had told me at the start.

Here are the things I actually learned, not the things the WWDC videos want you to believe you'll learn.

MVVM sounds clean until it isn't

Every SwiftUI tutorial will tell you to use MVVM. Very few will show you what happens when your view model starts doing too much, or when your data model needs to be shared between two view models that don't know about each other.

The real lesson: start with the simplest thing that works. You can always refactor into a cleaner architecture. You can't always refactor your way out of a mess you made trying to pre-architect for a scale you don't have.

The project file is the enemy

The .pbxproj file is Xcode's internal project configuration. It is a binary-adjacent format that has no mercy when you're trying to resolve merge conflicts or debug why a file you added three weeks ago isn't being compiled anymore.

My advice: commit often, and when something strange happens with missing files or linking errors, look at the project file first.

Dark mode is not an afterthought

If you build your app in light mode and add dark mode support later, you will touch almost every single view. Build dark mode support in from day one using semantic colors from the system palette — Color(.systemBackground), Color(.label), and so on — and you get dark mode for free.
